Background

Hercules was the son of Zeus, the king of the Greek gods. Hera, Zeus's wife, was jealous and drove Hercules mad — causing him to hurt his family. When Hercules recovered, he was filled with regret. He went to the Oracle of Delphi, who told him to serve King Eurystheus for twelve years and complete twelve labors. If he succeeded, he would become immortal and live with the gods on Mount Olympus.

The Twelve Labors
Labor 1
Slaying the Nemean Lion
The Nemean Lion had fur that no weapon could pierce. Hercules blocked one entrance to the lion's cave and entered through the other. He caught the lion and strangled it with his bare hands. He then wore the lion's skin as armour.
to slay to strangle impenetrable bare hands
slay = zabić (wroga/potwora)
strangle = udusić
impenetrable = nieprzenikalny
bare hands = gołe ręce
Labor 2
Slaying the Lernaean Hydra
The Hydra had 9 heads. Every time Hercules cut off one head, two more grew back. His nephew Iolaus helped by cauterizing (burning) each stump so no new heads could grow. Finally, Hercules cut off the immortal head with a golden sword.
to chop off to cauterize immortal to distract
chop off = odciąć
cauterize = przypalić (ranę), kauteryzować
immortal = nieśmiertelny
distract = rozpraszać uwagę
Labor 3
Capturing the Ceryneian Hind
Hercules had to capture (but not kill) a sacred golden deer belonging to the goddess Artemis. He waited until it was sleeping and caught it gently. Artemis was angry but forgave him when Hercules promised to return the deer.
to capture sacred golden antlers forgiveness
capture = schwytać
sacred = święty, poświęcony bogini
golden antlers = złote rogi (jelenia)
forgiveness = przebaczenie
Labor 4
Capturing the Erymanthian Boar
The Erymanthian Boar was a gigantic wild pig living on a mountain sacred to Artemis. Hercules chased it through deep snow until the boar was exhausted, then caught it in a net. King Eurystheus was so frightened that he hid inside a bronze jar!
boar to chase exhausted to hide
boar = dzik
chase = gonić, ścigać
exhausted = wyczerpany
hide = chować się
Labor 5
Cleaning the Augean Stables
The stables of King Augeas had thousands of animals and had never been cleaned in 30 years. Hercules diverted two rivers through the stables to wash them out — all in a single day! Eurystheus didn't count this labor because Hercules accepted payment.
stable to divert cattle to refuse
stable = stajnia
divert = przekierować (rzekę)
cattle = bydło
refuse = odmówić
Labor 6
Killing the Stymphalian Birds
These man-eating birds had metallic feathers and belonged to Ares, the god of war. Athena gave Hercules a rattle to scare them into the sky. He then shot them down with his poisonous arrows. He slayed 100 birds and the rest flew away.
metallic feathers rattle bow and arrow poisonous
metallic feathers = metalowe pióra
rattle = grzechotka
bow and arrow = łuk i strzała
poisonous = trujący
Labor 7
Capturing the Cretan Bull
The Cretan Bull was a gift from Poseidon (the sea god) to King Minos. It went wild and was destroying crops across Crete. Hercules wrestled it to the ground and tied it up. Later, the bull was tossed into the sky and became the constellation Taurus.
bull to wrestle wild / untamed constellation
bull = byk
wrestle = zapaśniczyć, rzucić na ziemię
wild = dziki, szalony
constellation = gwiazdozbiór
Labor 8
Stealing the Mares of Diomedes
King Diomedes owned four mares (female horses) trained to eat human flesh. They were so dangerous he kept them chained to a gold post. Hercules defeated Diomedes and fed him to his own horses. The horses calmed down and Hercules brought them safely to Eurystheus.
mare to chain to sneak up to tame
mare = klacz (samica konia)
chain = przykuć łańcuchem
sneak up = zakraść się
tame = oswajać, ujarzmić
Labor 9
Girdle of Hippolyta
Hercules had to get the magic girdle (belt) of Hippolyta, queen of the Amazons — a tribe of female warriors. Hippolyta was friendly and agreed to give it to him. But Hera spread rumours that Hercules planned to kidnap their queen. The Amazons attacked, and Hercules sailed away with the girdle.
girdle / belt warrior rumour to abduct
girdle / belt = pas, przepaska
warrior = wojownik/wojowniczka
rumour = plotka
abduct = porwać (kogoś)
Labor 10
Cattle of Geryon
Hercules traveled to the end of the earth to steal the red cattle of Geryon, a monster with three bodies. On his way, he shot an arrow at the sun god Helios — who instead of being angry, gave him a golden cup to help with the journey. Hercules defeated Geryon and eventually brought the cattle home.
cattle monster courage to retrieve
cattle = bydło, stado
monster = potwór
courage = odwaga
retrieve = odzyskać, przynieść z powrotem
Labor 11
Apples of the Hesperides
Hercules had to steal golden apples from a garden guarded by a dragon and nymphs. He found the Titan Atlas — who holds the sky on his shoulders — and tricked him. Hercules held the sky while Atlas fetched the apples, then cleverly tricked Atlas into taking the sky back.
golden apples to trick nymph Titan
golden apples = złote jabłka
trick = oszukać, przechytrzyć
nymph = nimfa
Titan = Tytan (rasa bogów-olbrzymów)
Labor 12
Capturing Cerberus
The final and hardest labor: capture Cerberus, the three-headed dog who guards the Underworld. Hercules learned secret knowledge to travel alive into the realm of the dead. He wrestled Cerberus with his bare hands. King Hades gave permission — and Eurystheus was so terrified he freed Hercules immediately.
Underworld guardian realm of the dead permission
Underworld = Podziemie, Hades
guardian = strażnik
realm of the dead = kraina umarłych
permission = pozwolenie
